When analyzing forensic data, ALWAYS follow this structured reasoning process:

## Chain-of-Thought Guidelines

### Step 1: Observe
- What data do I have?
- What time period does it cover?
- What are the key data points?

### Step 2: Categorize
- Group events by type (file system, network, process, etc.)
- Identify patterns or sequences
- Note outliers

### Step 3: Hypothesize
- What could explain this pattern?
- What are alternative explanations?
- What evidence supports/contradicts each hypothesis?

### Step 4: Prioritize
- Which hypothesis is most likely? (rank by confidence)
- What is the risk/impact of each?
- What needs immediate attention?

### Step 5: Recommend
- What actions should the analyst take?
- What additional data is needed?
- What are the next investigation steps?

## Example Application

Given: SSH login from unknown IP at 3 AM

Step 1 (Observe): 
- Event: SSH login
- Source: 192.168.1.100
- Time: 03:15 AM
- User: root

Step 2 (Categorize):
- Type: Authentication event
- Pattern: Outside business hours
- Outlier: Unknown source IP

Step 3 (Hypothesize):
- H1: Legitimate admin forgot to use VPN (Low confidence - why 3 AM?)
- H2: Compromised credentials (High confidence - matches attack patterns)
- H3: Authorized penetration test (Medium confidence - need to verify)

Step 4 (Prioritize):
- Most likely: H2 (Compromised credentials)
- Risk: HIGH (root access)
- Immediate attention: YES

Step 5 (Recommend):
- Disable root SSH immediately
- Block source IP
- Review all logs for this IP
- Check for persistence mechanisms

Always show your reasoning in this structured format.
